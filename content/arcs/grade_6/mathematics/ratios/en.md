> In Canada, maple syrup is a special product. The ratio of sugar to water when making syrup is 1 part sugar to 4 parts water. So if you use 1 cup of sugar, you need 4 cups of water. Now imagine a family wants to make 5 cups of syrup mix. How would they split the sugar and water?

## Stage 2: Concept

**Comprehension Check**

> What is the ratio of girls to boys in a class that has 9 girls and 12 boys?

A. 9:12  
B. 12:9  
C. 9 to 12  
D. 12 to 9  

**Intervention Hints:**

- **Level 1:** Which group comes first in the question? That's your first number.
- **Level 2:** Think of it like this: we compare girls to boys, which means we say "girls to boys."
- **Level 3:** If there are 9 girls and 12 boys, the ratio is written as "9 to 12" or "9:12."

**Correct Answer: A**

## Stage 3: Guided

**Guided Problem**

> A recipe calls for 3 cups of flour for every 2 cups of sugar. If you have 9 cups of flour, how many cups of sugar do you need?

A. 3 cups  
B. 5 cups  
C. 6 cups  
D. 9 cups  

**Intervention Hints:**

- **Level 1:** The flour and sugar are in a ratio of 3 to 2. You know how many cups of flour you have. What is the relationship?
- **Level 2:** 3 cups of flour = 2 cups of sugar. So, 9 cups of flour would be how many times 3?
- **Level 3:** 9 (flour) ÷ 3 = 3. So, multiply the sugar part by 3 too: 2 × 3 = 6 cups of sugar.

**Correct Answer: C**

## Stage 4: Practice

**Problem 1**

> In a race, a runner covers 150 meters in 30 seconds. What is her speed in meters per second?

A. 3 meters/second  
B. 5 meters/second  
C. 15 meters/second  
D. 4500 meters/second  

**Intervention Hints:**

- **Level 1:** Speed = Distance ÷ Time. You know the distance and the time.
- **Level 2:** Try calculating 150 ÷ 30.
- **Level 3:** Do the math: 150 ÷ 30 = 5. So, the speed is 5 meters/second.

**Correct Answer: B**

---

**Problem 2**

> In a class of 30 students, 18 are wearing blue shirts, and 12 are wearing red shirts. What is the ratio of blue to red shirts?

A. 18:12  
B. 12:18  
C. 18 to 30  
D. 12 to 30  

**Intervention Hints:**

- **Level 1:** The question asks for blue to red shirts, not blue to total.
- **Level 2:** Think – how many of each kind? Blue is 18, red is 12.
- **Level 3:** The ratio is blue to red: 18:12.

**Correct Answer: A**

---

**Problem 3**

> A car travels 120 kilometers in 2 hours. What is the unit rate?

A. 60 km/2 hours  
B. 120 km/2 hours  
C. 60 km/hour  
D. 120 km/hour  

**Intervention Hints:**

- **Level 1:** The unit rate means we want the distance for 1 hour only.
- **Level 2:** Divide 120 km by 2 hours to get the distance per hour.
- **Level 3:** 120 ÷ 2 = 60. So, 60 km/hour is the unit rate.

**Correct Answer: C**
