## Stage 1: Hook

**Hook Story 1 – Temperature in Canada**

It's a cold winter morning in Montreal. The temperature starts at negative 5 degrees Celsius. By noon, it has warmed up by 8 degrees. What is the temperature now?

**Hook Story 2 – Elevation in the Canadian Rockies**

Sarah is hiking in the Canadian Rockies. She starts at 1000 meters above sea level. She descends down to 300 meters. What is her final elevation?

**Hook Purpose**

## Stage 2: Concept

### Concept Introduction: Integers

Integers are positive and negative whole numbers. They include zero. The number line helps us see how integers work. For example:
- Positive numbers go to the right (e.g., 3)
- Negative numbers go to the left (e.g., -3)
- Zero is in the middle

We can use the number line to compare, add, and subtract integers.

### Comprehension Check:

**Question:** Which of the following shows a negative integer?

A) 8  
B) 0  
C) -5  
D) 12  

**Answer:** C) -5  
**Intervention Hints:**

**Level 1:** Think about numbers that are below zero.  
**Level 2:** On the number line, which numbers are to the left of zero?  
**Level 3:** Which number in the list is less than zero?

## Stage 3: Guided

### Guided Problem: Ordering Integers

**Problem:**  
Order the following integers from least to greatest: -3, 5, -7, 2

**Answer Choices:**  
A) -3, -7, 2, 5  
B) -7, -3, 2, 5  
C) 5, 2, -3, -7  
D) -7, 2, -3, 5  

**Answer:** B) -7, -3, 2, 5  
**Intervention Hints:**

**Level 1:** Draw a number line and place each integer on it.  
**Level 2:** Which number is the furthest to the left?  
**Level 3:** Start from the smallest number and move to the right on the number line.

## Stage 4: Practice

### Practice Problem 1: Adding Integers

**Problem:**  
A submarine is at a depth of 20 meters below sea level. It ascends 15 meters. What is its new depth?

A) 35 meters below sea level  
B) 5 meters below sea level  
C) 5 meters above sea level  
D) 35 meters above sea level  

**Answer:** B) 5 meters below sea level  
**Intervention Hints:**

**Level 1:** Start at -20 on the number line. Move up 15 units.  
**Level 2:** Think: Depth below sea level is represented by negative numbers.  
**Level 3:** What is -20 + 15?

### Practice Problem 2: Subtracting Integers

**Problem:**  
The temperature in Calgary was -10°C in the morning. By evening it had dropped by 5°C. What was the evening temperature?

A) -15°C  
B) -5°C  
C) 5°C  
D) 15°C  

**Answer:** A) -15°C  
**Intervention Hints:**

**Level 1:** Start at -10 on the number line. Move left 5 units.  
**Level 2:** To subtract a positive number is like moving left on the number line.  
**Level 3:** What is -10 - 5?

### Practice Problem 3: Real-World Application

**Problem:**  
Jack has a bank account balance of $200. He withdraws $300. What is his new balance?

A) $500  
B) $100  
C) -$100  
D) -$500  

**Answer:** C) -$100  
**Intervention Hints:**

**Level 1:** Think of it as moving along a number line. Start at +200.  
**Level 2:** Withdrawing money is like subtracting a positive amount.  
**Level 3:** What is 200 - 300?

## Stage 5: Capstone

### Capstone Problem:

**Problem:**  
In a ski resort, the temperature in the morning was -5°C. It rose 12°C by noon and then dropped 8°C by evening. What was the temperature in the evening?

**Answer Choices:**
A) -1°C  
B) 1°C  
C) -21°C  
D) 21°C  

**Answer:** A) -1°C  
**Solution Steps:**
1. Start at -5°C
2. Add 12°C: -5 + 12 = 7°C
3. Subtract 8°C: 7 - 8 = -1°C

**Intervention Hints (before second attempt only):**  
**Level 1:** Show each step on a number line.  
**Level 2:** Remember: adding a positive number moves right; subtracting a positive number moves left.  
**Level 3:** What is the temperature after the rise? What is it after the drop?
